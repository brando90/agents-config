## Distilled rules (as encoded in write-poster.md)

1. **Three essential parts: motivation, clear intuitive results, takeaway.** Everything else is optional support.
2. **Audience model: the exhausted walker.** Design for a tired attendee shuffling past after hours on the poster floor, not a fresh referee reading at a desk.
3. **"Make it so stupidly obvious."** If the claim requires effort to extract, the poster failed — obviousness is the acceptance test.
4. **Figures over text everywhere.** Motivation, results, and takeaway should each be carried by a figure/image wherever possible; big text blocks are exhausting (Brando's corollary, endorsed by Rylan).
